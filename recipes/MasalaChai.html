Masala Chai

Description

Indian masala chai is a traditional, aromatic, and spiced tea beverage that originated in India. It combines black tea leaves (usually Assam or Darjeeling) with a blend of spices, milk, and a sweetener.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 2 tsp black tea leaves or 2 tea bags (Assam or Darjeeling tea)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, crushed
  • 2 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 2 cloves
  • 1/4 tsp fennel seeds (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp black peppercorns (optional)
  • 2 tsp sugar, or to taste (can also use honey or other sweeteners)

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, combine water, crushed ginger, crushed cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, cloves, fennel seeds (if using), and black peppercorns (if using). Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Allow the mixture to boil for 2-3 minutes, so the flavors of the spices infuse into the water.
  3. Add black tea leaves or tea bags to the saucepan and let it boil for another 1-2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the milk and stir gently. Bring the mixture back to a gentle boil and then reduce the heat to low, letting it simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add sugar or your preferred sweetener, stirring well to dissolve it.
  6. Turn off the heat and let the chai steep for a minute or two to enhance the flavors.
  7. Strain the masala chai through a fine mesh strainer into cups or a teapot, discarding the solids.
  8. Serve the masala chai hot, with biscuits or snacks of your choice.
